Human Economy

A human economy is one where money is used primarily as a way of reorganizing social relationships and not as a means of exchanging goods and services. The term was coined by David Graeber in his book Toward an Anthropological Theory of Value. He continued to write about the idea in Debt:the first 5,000 years and his essay All Economies Are Ultimately Human Economies.
